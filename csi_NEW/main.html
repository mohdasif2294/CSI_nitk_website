About Us

Our Mission

To facilitate knowledge sharing, learning and career enhancement encompassing all streams of engineering and thus, better the quality of technical education.

The CSI- NITK plans and organizes a wide range of events that include technical workshops, guest lectures, online events and various other competitions at NITK, throughout the year. CSI-NITK does not just limit itself to computer science and allied fields. Our members come from all disciplines , backgrounds and interest to make it a melting pot. CSI has worked closely with ENGINEER right from the time of its inception to help make it into south India's second biggest technical symposium. A part of the NITK establishment for over a decade, CSI has been a forum for students to create projects, discuss upcoming ideas and technologies, develop organisational and leadership skills, and find like-minded students to work with.

Yantras

Yantras are special interest groups within CSI NITK. They pursue individual projects and hold knowledge exchange programs. Yantras provide for focused interaction and a better learning experience.

Sanganitra

The computer science interest group is one of the broadest categories of work at CSI. Unofficial Coding contests were held on a fortnightly basis to keep the enthusiasm intact. Besides, regular Java and Python sessions were held.

Vidyut

Dealing with electrical and electronics engineering, the purview of Vidyut ranges from robotics to signal processing to machine learning and other important fields. Open CV sessions for gesture recognition and using FANN libraries were conducted .Projects like PID controller taken up and completed .

Yantrika

Working in the field of mechanical engineering, Yantrika straddles fields such as robotics and rocketry. A workshop on how to build and go about working with a Radio Controlled model IC engine car.

Kaaryavatra

To come together and learn about the fields of finance, marketing and entrepreneurship. We discuss case studies and current affairs to understand markets work.

Projects

CSI-NITK is defined by the innovative and challenging projects that every yantra works on. So far, this academic year saw CSI-NITK pursue the following projects:

POV Project

POV- Persistence of Vision), works on the principle that our brain cannot distinguish between images that are less than 0.04 seconds apart. The idea is to rotate an LED-Matrix so fast that it creates a continuum of images which the mind processes and super-imposes to be one.

Raspberry Pi GUI

The Raspberry Pi is a credit card sized computer manufactured for students to learn electronics.The aim of this project is to use a GUI on the laptop's core that would give access to all the functionality on the Raspberry Pi's ARM11 core. The GUI uses a virtual network to display the Raspbian OS on the laptop.

Pipe-Climbing robot

A robot that can climb up vertical surfaces with the help of an architectural feature such as a pipe. It’s proven to be very useful in unconventional avenues that vary as widely as photography and utility/maintenance.

Thermoelectric Fan

This project aspires to exploit the See beck effect (which correlates the direct conversion from temperature difference to potential difference and vice-versa). The effect is thereon used to produce a fan that can function at 300 rpm using a commonly available heat source such as a candle.

Activities

At CSI members are not focused only on technical competence but believe in holistic development. CSI-NITK aims to give back to the community through various initatives.

Granthalaya

A volunteer program initiated to assist the library staff in organising and developing the NITK library. Monthly clean ups and daily organisation are key components of this program. The Library awareness and clean-up programme took place during the course of the year where CSI members, in collaboration with library staff, did their part in ensuring that the books were re-arranged and kept in their correct places.

Shiksha

Shiksha-our computer literacy awareness program for school children has grown to be a successful initiative by CSI-NITK members. Under the Shiksha banner, volunteers from CSI-NITK, currently teach 8th and 9th standard students of the KREC Kannada Medium School for one hour on every Saturday respectively.

Fests And Events

INNOVISION(Tech Fest)

Innovision provides an environment conducive for innovation, creativity, team-spirit and entrepreneurial leadership. This technical festival of CSI-NITK, usually held during the last week of August, provides a platform for students to apply their technical knowledge and skills in real life.

Events held during INNOVISION’13

Cryptick

Conducted by Sanganitra, the event was designed to trick the minds of the participants and consisted of three rounds. Starting with a quiz based on logic and common knowledge, and moving on to a live board game, this event saw intense lateral thinking and team work. The final round was based on the idea of stopping a bomb from detonating which tested the quality and quantity of the work, given a time constraint to emphasise on “The essence of every tick”. …

Jugaad

Organised by the Yantrika, participants in teams of 3 had to use their technical knowhow coupled with their "Jugaad" skills to solve the given problem statements. In the round ‘Contraption’, the participating teams had to complete a set of tasks, by building a contraption model using basic materials. The final round included building a hydraulic crane from scratch, providing a platform for thinking out of the box and arriving at innovative solutions. …

Hardwired

This Tronix-based event conducted by Vidyut comprised of logic based tests and designing basic digital and analog circuits on a breadboard with simple components like resistors, LEDs, logic gate ICs. The final round involved teammates communicating through electronic Morse code. Most of the teams had not implemented electronic circuits and were fascinated and eager to learn. We had a small session prior to the event to explain some basic concepts, so they took back something worthwhile after the event. …

Even Semester Events

Apprentice

The Apprentice consists largely of 2 rounds: the selection of prospective candidates and the finals during which the candidates encounter team activities and face the difficulties of having to build a product from scratch and then adequately market it. Apart from heart-warming ice breaker sessions, every round is used as a sieve to pick out the best among the lot, who then individually face a panel of judges as they defend their solutions to problem statements given to them.…

Around the world in 127 minutes

The name of the event can be attributed to the combination of Literature, Geography and Films, a first for any NITK event! Each genre was associated with a country - such as Australia for Sports, India for Current affairs - and the countries were spread all around the campus. Teams were provided with a map, manuscript and passport to guide them on their quest. Teams had to complete tasks in each country to earn miles and resources/artefacts (diamonds, scrolls, paintings, spices etc.). …

Big Fight

CSI's The Big Fight is an event in which the\finalists have the opportunity develop a solution to a problem they face in college . The next round is aimed to divide the finalists into three teams, finalise their area of focus and initiate team building. The final round consisted of the three teams presenting their ideas before a panel of judges consisting of well-known professors, followed by a question & answer session to test the feasibility of their idea, research etc. …

Street View

Google Street View was yet another master innovision by Google, one of the major IT companies of the era. It involved a delve into the actual streets of a particular area giving the user an actual feel of being a part of the place itself. Emulation of exactly this product of Google was attempted and the area for the view chosen was our college itself. It was completed for some of the places in college and will be carried over to the next academic year for it to be extended to an area spanning over the complete college.
